City Riding

City riding refers to riding a bicycle or any other vehicle within an urban environment, such as a city or a town. It involves navigating through crowded streets, negotiating traffic, and dealing with various road and weather conditions, among other challenges. City riding can also refer to the style of riding, which often involves riding shorter distances at lower speeds, compared to riding in rural areas or on open roads. This type of riding requires a different set of skills and techniques to ensure safety and efficiency. City riding can be a great way to commute, exercise, and explore a city while reducing carbon footprint.

Freestyle Riding

Freestyle riding is a type of cycling that involves performing tricks and stunts on a variety of terrain, including ramps, jumps, and obstacles. It is often associated with BMX (Bicycle Motocross) and involves a wide range of acrobatic maneuvers, such as aerials, spins, grinds, and flips. Freestyle riding requires a high level of skill, balance, and coordination, as well as a willingness to take risks. Outdoor Riding

Outdoor riding refers to cycling in the open air, typically on roads or off-road trails that are not in a controlled environment like a velodrome or an indoor cycling studio. Outdoor riding can include a variety of cycling disciplines, such as road cycling, mountain biking, cyclocross, gravel riding, and touring. It can take place in a range of natural environments, from mountains and forests to deserts and coastal areas. Outdoor riding can be done for a variety of reasons, including fitness, recreation, competition, commuting, and touring.
